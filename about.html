About

I am a Ph.D. candidate in Geosciences at the University of Arizona where my research focuses on using seismic and potential fields data in concert with numerical modeling to resolve geologic histories.

At a more general level, I am interested in all aspects related to modeling and programming in the Geosciences. I believe that there is a lot of value to be gained by being able to model, analyze, and (perhaps most importantly) visualize geologic phenomena easily and efficiently.

When I am not nerding out at the computer, I enjoy watching baseball, hiking in the mountains, cycling, and cooking. There is a fine balance with everything!
